About the workshop

The PhD workshop on Advances in Causal Inference Methods is dedicated to exploring the latest developments and applications in the field. This workshop provides an exceptional platform for both early career and established researchers to engage intensively with new methodologies in applied microeconomics. We aim to cultivate a collaborative and interactive environment, encouraging the exchange of ideas, networking, and scholarly development. Participants are invited to present their research through oral presentations or posters, utilizing causal inference techniques across diverse microeconomic topics. Each presentation will benefit from the insights of a senior scholar who will serve as a discussant, offering tailored, constructive feedback. Our agenda also features presentations from two distinguished keynote speakers who will shed light on cutting-edge advancements in causal inference. Additionally, the workshop will include dedicated time slots for one-on-one discussions with keynote speakers, allowing attendees to delve deeper into specific areas of interest and gain expert advice on their research endeavors. This setting is ideal for PhD students eager to advance their understanding and application of causal inference in their work, fostering meaningful connections and collaborations within the academic community.
