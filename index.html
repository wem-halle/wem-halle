About the workshop

The PhD workshop on Advances in Causal Inference Methods is dedicated to exploring the latest developments and applications in the field. This workshop provides a platform for early career to engage with senior researchers on new methodologies in applied microeconomics. We aim to cultivate a collaborative and interactive environment, encouraging the exchange of ideas, networking, and scholarly development.

The workshop will focus on research that utilizes causal inference techniques across diverse microeconomic topics. Our agenda also features presentations from two distinguished keynote speakers who will shed light on cutting-edge advancements in causal inference. Additionally, the workshop will include dedicated time slots for one-on-one discussions with keynote speakers, allowing attendees to delve deeper into specific areas of interest and gain expert advice on their research projects. This setting is ideal for PhD students eager to advance their understanding and application of causal inference in their work, fostering meaningful connections and collaborations within the academic community.
